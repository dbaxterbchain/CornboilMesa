Beanchain Corn Boil

Every fall, the Beanchain community gathers around one giant pot to share steaming corn, warm stories, and generous hearts. Together we raise funds to fight poverty and ensure every neighbor has a full plate.

What is the Corn Boil?

The Beanchain Corn Boil is our signature autumn tradition. We roast and boil locally grown sweet corn, blend it with heirloom beans, and serve it alongside live music, artisan vendors, and hands-on activities for all ages. Proceeds benefit community-led programs that provide food security, job training, and educational resources to families experiencing poverty.

Local Harvest

We source corn from Beanchain farmers and cook it in the town square, filling the crisp fall air with the aroma of butter and spice.

Shared Table

Neighbors, friends, and visitors gather around communal tables to enjoy hearty bowls of corn and beans, freshly baked bread, and seasonal soups.

Giving Back

Every ticket, donation, and volunteer hour supports initiatives that uplift families and tackle the root causes of poverty in Beanchain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does the event take place?

The Corn Boil is hosted at Beanchain Commons, 123 Market Street. Bring a blanket or lawn chair to enjoy the live performances.

Is the event family-friendly?

Absolutely! We offer a dedicated kids' activity zone, face painting, and corn-themed crafts throughout the day.

How can I support if I cannot attend?

You can make a tax-deductible donation, sponsor a family, or sign up for a volunteer shift at a partner organization year-round.

What should I bring?

Dress in cozy layers, bring reusable utensils if you have them, and carry a big appetite. We will have refill stations for water bottles.
